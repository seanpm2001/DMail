About DMail

Intro

Dmail is the default email client for SNU. It is highly customizable, and has compatibility with several other email clients.

As an acronym

Dmail stands for:

Digital Mail

Revival

When the idea for Dmail was first created, it was not intended for SNU. It was going to be its own separate project. At the time, I was only working on assets. I didn't have the capability of making it a thing yet.

On March 24th 2019, Sean decided to bring the project to SNU. He went on a hard drive search that took about 20 minutes to get the original assets (over 1 gigabyte in size) throughout the day, minor changes were made, such as an inbox, welcome center, import center, compose center, about page, and import center.

It is still worked on to this day, as of March 24th 2019, not at a frequent rate compared to the rest of the site

Offline

One of the benefits of DMail is you have access to your entire inbox when you are online if you load it first. You can compose Emails, but you can't send them. You can also adjust settings

Connection

DMail doesn't use that much data, it is slim and powerful. However, when you turn on fancy graphics, and adjust advanced settings, you might need to get something better than 3G to continue running smoothly. If you have 4G, 5G, it will work fine. Let's not forget Wi-Fi, make sure you have at least 450 kbps available for Dmail on your device.

Domain and address

With Dmail, you get to choose your own custom domain without paying any money (unlike gmail) if you don't choose a domain, you will get an email defaulted either like one of these:

newuser@dmail.com

newuser@dmail.net

newuser@snumail.com

newuser@snumail.net

Also, your domain for your email can be up to 256 characters before the @ and 32 characters after the @, making your email address max out at 289 characters. Make sure to make it easy to remember or write it down, an address that long is difficult to manage mentally.

You can use special characters in your domain, however, they must be either ANSI, ASCII, UTF-2, UTF-8, UTF-16, or any in between

Max email size

Dmail supports VERY large emails. However, we have to put a limit on how big a single email is.

Dmail supports emails up to 2 Gigabytes in size. We don't recommend sending large emails frequently, as your account can run out of space. Someday, when technology improves, we will increase the size limit.
